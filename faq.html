Frequently Asked Questions

Is this some elaborate scam?

No! I care about earning your trust while we do business together. While I understand that you may be skeptical about responding to strangers who sent you a letter in the mail, I operate my business in a professional, courteous, and trustworthy manner. I really do want to buy your land. If you still don’t believe me, email Reed@landflare.com and I will prove to you that I am a real person with a genuine interest in real estate.

How did you get my information?

We get all of our ownership records from the county assessor’s database. Each county maintains a public record of who owns what properties. They use this information to send you tax bills. We use it to send you offers to purchase your land. The information is publicly available to anyone who asks for it. However, we do not share your personal information with any third party data collectors or advertisers.

How do you calculate offer prices?

We consider several different factors when setting offer prices for different properties. These factors include the 4 “A’s”: access, acreage, amenities (like utilities, water, trees, etc), and affordability (or similar market conditions).

I owe property taxes. Will you still purchase my property?

Maybe! Most of the time we can pay back property taxes (within reason). Even if you owe a lot, we can still negotiate a win-win situation.

I inherited my property. Can I still sell it?

Each situation is different, but generally inherited property can be sold just as easily. Certain factors, however, may make it easier. If there have been probate or other testamentary proceedings in court, we might be able to close faster than if we have to probate your relatives’ estate. If you don’t know whether there has been probate (or an affidavit of heirship, etc.), ask your attorney to help you.

Why should I sell to you?

We offer a headache-free land sales experience. You don’t have to worry about marketing your land on various MLS listings or websites; you don’t have to hire a real estate agent; and you don’t have to worry about “days on market.” We can get money in your pocket in as little as a week, without the middle-men. The offer price that we agree on is the amount on the check we give you. It’s that simple.

Do I need to hire an attorney?

While we would never discourage someone from seeking legal counsel, attorneys are not necessary to complete transactions with us. Our contracts are written in simple, easy-to-understand terms, and we don’t believe in “gotcha” small print. However, if you would feel more comfortable hiring an attorney, please do so! We would love to speak with them if necessary.

Is this "Purchase Agreement" a real contract?

The letters we send to people contain an enclosed document entitled “Purchase Agreement.” Once you sign it, it becomes a legally binding offer for us to purchase your property. We reserve the right to decline transactions based on our investment criteria, available funds for purchase, title deficiencies, or any other factor we deem appropriate.

Where are you located?

While we purchase property all over the country, we set up shop in the Portland Oregon area. Due to the amazing technology available to us via the internet, we are able to purchase and sell land from virtually anywhere.

What do you do with all the land you purchase?

Some of it I keep for personal use, but most of it I market and sell to targeted recreational users and developers.

Get in touch
